## Venkatesh Immadi — Business Intelligence Lead

I'm a BI Lead with 10+ years of experience building and running analytics platforms across some of the most data-intensive industries in the world — healthcare, financial services, and enterprise tech. I'm based in Bengaluru, India.

My work sits at the intersection of data engineering, visual analytics, and business stakeholder management. I've led teams, owned production deployments, and spent a lot of time thinking about how to make data actually useful — not just technically correct, but interpretable, fast, and trusted by the people who make decisions with it.

### What I Do

At **GE Healthcare**, I lead BI and data warehouse delivery for a global analytics platform that tracks medical device usage — CT, MRI, MG, and Ultrasound — across hospital networks in 18 countries. The platform runs on AWS and on-premises, serves 100+ hospital users, and is delivered in 18 languages. My responsibilities span dashboard development, Tableau Server administration, AWS infrastructure, GitLab CI/CD governance, and L4 production incident resolution.

Before that, at **Oracle Financial Services**, I delivered compliance-grade BI for two demanding financial clients: an AML customer screening platform for Citi Bank (row-level security for 500+ users, PII data controls), and a securities portfolio analytics system for State Street (benchmarking against Aladdin, Bloomberg, and MSCI).

At **Brillio / Dell**, I built marketing performance dashboards used by Dell's VP of Marketing to track actuals, forecasts, and outlook across consumer, SMB, gaming, and retail markets globally. I also led a Tableau-to-Power BI migration under tight timelines and stakeholder sign-off pressure.

At **Capgemini**, I cut my teeth on a large-scale procurement data warehouse for Baker Hughes/GE — Greenplum-based, Oracle + SAP ERP integration, Informatica ETL, and the full stack of what enterprise BI actually looks like at 30+ stakeholders.

### What I'm Exploring

The BI tooling landscape is shifting fast. Tableau MCP, Power BI Copilot, Databricks + dbt, AI-assisted SQL generation — these are changing what "BI work" means. I'm writing about this here as I explore it: what actually works, what's hype, and what a decade of production BI experience tells me about where this is going.
